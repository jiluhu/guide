# Option Strangle (Long Strangle)


# 选项扼杀（长扼杀）
The long strangle, also known as buy strangle or simply "strangle", is a neutral strategy in options trading that involve the simultaneous    buying of a slightly out-of-the-money put    and a slightly out-of-the-money call    of the same underlying stock and expiration date. 

b'\xe9\x95\xbf\xe6\x9c\x9f\xe6\x89\xbc\xe6\x9d\x80\xef\xbc\x8c\xe4\xb9\x9f\xe8\xa2\xab\xe7\xa7\xb0\xe4\xb8\xba\xe4\xb9\xb0\xe5\x85\xa5\xe6\x89\xbc\xe6\x9d\x80\xe6\x88\x96\xe7\xae\x80\xe5\x8d\x95\xe5\x9c\xb0\xe2\x80\x9c\xe6\x89\xbc\xe6\x9d\x80\xe2\x80\x9d\xef\xbc\x8c\xe5\x9c\xa8\xe6\x9c\x9f\xe6\x9d\x83\xe4\xba\xa4\xe6\x98\x93\xe4\xb8\xad\xe6\x98\xaf\xe4\xb8\x80\xe7\xa7\x8d\xe4\xb8\xad\xe6\x80\xa7\xe7\xad\x96\xe7\x95\xa5\xef\xbc\x8c\xe6\xb6\x89\xe5\x8f\x8a\xe5\x90\x8c\xe6\x97\xb6\xe4\xb9\xb0\xe5\x85\xa5\xe7\x95\xa5\xe5\xbe\xae\xe4\xbb\xb7\xe5\xa4\x96\xe7\x9a\x84\xe7\x9c\x8b\xe8\xb7\x8c\xe6\x9c\x9f\xe6\x9d\x83\xe5\x92\x8c\xe7\x95\xa5\xe5\xbe\xae\xe4\xb8\x8d\xe5\xae\x9e\xe9\x99\x85\xe7\x9a\x84\xe7\x9c\x8b\xe6\xb6\xa8\xe6\x9c\x9f\xe6\x9d\x83\xe3\x80\x82'
The long options strangle is an unlimited profit, limited risk strategy that is taken when    the options trader thinks that the underlying stock will experience significant    volatility in the near term. Long strangles are debit        spreads as a net debit is taken to enter the trade.

b'\xe9\x95\xbf\xe6\x9c\x9f\xe6\x9c\x9f\xe6\x9d\x83\xe6\x89\xbc\xe6\x9d\x80\xe6\x98\xaf\xe4\xb8\x80\xe7\xa7\x8d\xe6\x97\xa0\xe9\x99\x90\xe7\x9a\x84\xe5\x88\xa9\xe6\xb6\xa6\xef\xbc\x8c\xe6\x9c\x89\xe9\x99\x90\xe7\x9a\x84\xe9\xa3\x8e\xe9\x99\xa9\xe7\xad\x96\xe7\x95\xa5\xef\xbc\x8c\xe5\xbd\x93\xe6\x9c\x9f\xe6\x9d\x83\xe4\xba\xa4\xe6\x98\x93\xe8\x80\x85\xe8\xae\xa4\xe4\xb8\xba\xe5\x9f\xba\xe7\xa1\x80\xe8\x82\xa1\xe7\xa5\xa8\xe5\xb0\x86\xe5\x9c\xa8\xe7\x9f\xad\xe6\x9c\x9f\xe5\x86\x85\xe7\xbb\x8f\xe5\x8e\x86\xe9\x87\x8d\xe5\xa4\xa7\xe6\xb3\xa2\xe5\x8a\xa8\xe6\x97\xb6\xe9\x87\x87\xe5\x8f\x96\xe3\x80\x82'

## Limited Risk


## 风险有限
Maximum loss for the long strangle options strategy is hit when the underlying stock price on expiration date is trading    between the strike prices of the options bought. At this price, both options expire worthless    and the options trader loses the entire initial debit taken to enter the trade.

b'\xe5\xbd\x93\xe5\x88\xb0\xe6\x9c\x9f\xe6\x97\xa5\xe7\x9a\x84\xe6\xa0\x87\xe7\x9a\x84\xe8\x82\xa1\xe7\xa5\xa8\xe4\xbb\xb7\xe6\xa0\xbc\xe5\x9c\xa8\xe6\x89\x80\xe8\xb4\xad\xe4\xb9\xb0\xe6\x9c\x9f\xe6\x9d\x83\xe7\x9a\x84\xe6\x89\xa7\xe8\xa1\x8c\xe4\xbb\xb7\xe6\xa0\xbc\xe4\xb9\x8b\xe9\x97\xb4\xe4\xba\xa4\xe6\x98\x93\xe6\x97\xb6\xef\xbc\x8c\xe5\x8f\xaf\xe4\xbb\xa5\xe8\x8e\xb7\xe5\xbe\x97\xe9\x95\xbf\xe6\x9c\x9f\xe6\x89\xbc\xe6\x9d\x80\xe6\x9c\x9f\xe6\x9d\x83\xe7\xad\x96\xe7\x95\xa5\xe7\x9a\x84\xe6\x9c\x80\xe5\xa4\xa7\xe6\x8d\x9f\xe5\xa4\xb1\xe3\x80\x82'

## Example


## 例
    Suppose XYZ stock is trading at $40 in June. An options trader executes a long strangle    by buying a JUL 35 put for $100 and a JUL 45 call for $100. The net debit taken to enter the trade    is $200, which is also his maximum possible loss.

b'\xe5\x81\x87\xe8\xae\xbeXYZ\xe8\x82\xa1\xe7\xa5\xa86\xe6\x9c\x88\xe4\xba\xa4\xe6\x98\x93\xe4\xbb\xb7\xe6\xa0\xbc\xe4\xb8\xba40\xe7\xbe\x8e\xe5\x85\x83\xe3\x80\x82'
        If XYZ stock rallies and is trading at $50 on expiration in July, the JUL 35 put will expire        worthless but the JUL 45 call expires in the money and has an intrinsic value        of $500. Subtracting the initial debit of $200, the options trader's profit        comes to $300.    

b'\xe5\xa6\x82\xe6\x9e\x9cXYZ\xe8\x82\xa1\xe7\xa5\xa8\xe5\x9c\xa87\xe6\x9c\x88\xe5\x88\xb0\xe6\x9c\x9f\xe6\x97\xb6\xe5\x8f\x8d\xe5\xbc\xb9\xe5\xb9\xb6\xe4\xb8\x94\xe4\xba\xa4\xe6\x98\x93\xe4\xbb\xb7\xe6\xa0\xbc\xe4\xb8\xba50\xe7\xbe\x8e\xe5\x85\x83\xef\xbc\x8c\xe5\x88\x99JUL 35\xe7\x9c\x8b\xe8\xb7\x8c\xe6\x9c\x9f\xe6\x9d\x83\xe5\xb0\x86\xe6\x97\xa0\xe6\x95\x88\xef\xbc\x8c\xe4\xbd\x86JUL 45\xe7\x9c\x8b\xe6\xb6\xa8\xe6\x9c\x9f\xe6\x9d\x83\xe5\xb0\x86\xe5\x9c\xa8\xe8\xb4\xa7\xe5\xb8\x81\xe5\x88\xb0\xe6\x9c\x9f\xe5\xb9\xb6\xe5\x85\xb7\xe6\x9c\x89500\xe7\xbe\x8e\xe5\x85\x83\xe7\x9a\x84\xe5\x86\x85\xe5\x9c\xa8\xe4\xbb\xb7\xe5\x80\xbc\xe3\x80\x82'
        On expiration in July, if XYZ stock is still trading at $40, both the JUL 35 put        and the JUL 45 call expire worthless and the options        trader suffers a maximum loss which is equal to the initial debit of $200 taken        to enter the trade.
